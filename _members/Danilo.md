    Danilo Bórquez-Paredes earned his Ph.D. in Complex Systems Engineering from Universidad Adolfo Ibáñez in 2018. He is currently an Assistant Professor at the School of Engineering and Sciences at UAI in Viña del Mar, Chile. His research focuses on optical networks and telecommunications systems, with an emphasis on resource allocation, network optimization, and discrete-event simulation. He develops high-performance simulation tools to evaluate network behavior and support the design of efficient algorithms. His work bridges theory and practice in areas such as quality of service (QoS), network virtualization, and distributed protocols, contributing to the advancement of next-generation communication systems.
